The Studio

Where form
meets intention

Forma is a one-person ceramics studio tucked in the foothills of the Pacific Northwest. Every piece begins as a conversation between hands and earth — shaped on the wheel, finished by feel, and fired in a wood kiln that breathes unpredictability into each glaze.

We make functional work that invites daily use: vessels for flowers, bowls for morning ritual, cups that warm the hands in winter. Nothing here is made twice. Nothing here is made fast.

How we work

The Process

01

Earth & Intention

Every piece begins with reclaimed stoneware clay sourced from local deposits. We wedge by hand — a ritual that centers both the material and the maker before throwing begins.

02

Wheel & Fire

Thrown on a kick wheel, each form is pulled slowly, dried under cloth for days, then bisque-fired before the glaze is applied by brush, pour, or dip — never twice the same way.

03

Release & Rest

A final wood-kiln firing brings ash and flame into contact with the glaze — a collaboration we cannot fully control. The kiln decides the final surface. We accept what it gives.
